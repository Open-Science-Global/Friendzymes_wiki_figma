Project

Just Four labs.

One ambitious goal.

It all came together

Here we describe the intricate ways through which our project came together and the future that we envision after. We take you through the hurdles that are currently prevalent in being a biohacker of the 21st century and the ways we’ve smoothened the road through our proof of concept and implementation suggestions. We also let you meet the amazing people who directly and indirectly played a part in making our vision come to life.
SoftWare

A desire to optimize.

Marathon coding sessions.

The ultimate software solution.

Here we describe the software tools that make it possible to easily optimize DNA/RNA designs and deal with open-source hardware. We take you through the need for the software we created, dive deep into its functionality and familiarize you with some scenarios(tutorials) that will easily enable you to get started right away for most of your enzyme production needs.
WetWare

An inefficient traditional way.

A not-so subtle change.

We engineered cultures of B.subtilis.

Here we describe the process of our wetware constructions and list down the parts we were successful in engineering. We take you through the protocols we followed to make our microbes secrete our desired enzyme and the safety we ensured while doing it.
Hardware

Easy access, open source tools for rescue.

We tinkered it together

Here we describe the minimal hardware setups required to have your own frugal biofoundry/DIY biolab up and running. We take you through the hardware components needed, frugally get your hands on the requirements and efficiently deal with the hurdles you will face during the build.
